FLOE Seminar

Financial Levers for Operational Effects Seminar

Course Description

The FLOE seminar teaches participants how to understand and apply financial information and intelligence to support and achieve organizational effects and end states – both offensively and defensively. Students learn key concepts of preventing ‘friendly’ financial efforts from negatively impacting operations and our strategic objectives (defense), the economic impacts of the local Operating Environment, as well as exploiting and understanding adversarial efforts to raise, store, move and use financial resources to achieve desired effects (offense) working within an inter-agency and international framework.

    End State: Students Understand

  1. How financial intelligence connects organizational people, operations, logistics and networks.
  2. How ‘value’ and resourcing is generated, stored, moved and used and what opportunities or threats it can present for their mission set.
  3. How to employ financial information to achieve operational effects.
  4. How do protect their own unit’s financial and logistical efforts.
  5. Inter-Agency and International perspectives, authorities and capabilities – and the necessity for a team-oriented approach spanning partner agencies.
  6. The near-universal application of financial information to all occupational specialties and units.
  7. The economic impacts of the operating environment upon military end states as well as military impacts to the local economy.

A little about me

Brian is a zealot for and training organizations in the unique application of financial information, processes, organizations and understanding as they apply to National Security to achieve desired outcomes, effects and end states. He is an innovator with a passion for pioneering, developing and applying Financial Levers in both conventional and unconventional manners to safeguard resources and to understand adversarial resourcing processes.

Brian’s financial and National Security perspectives were honed and informed by multiple military operational tours serving in Iraq, Afghanistan, Kuwait, and Bosnia, two tours in Germany and direct support to Irregular Warfare, Counter Terrorism and Special Forces mission sets.

Brian is a retired Army Finance and Comptroller Colonel with over 28 years of experience spanning Counter Threat Finance, Resource Management, Accounting, Disbursing and Comptrollership. He spent over 12 years serving within the Special Operations community in the U.S. Special Operations Command (USSOCOM), U.S. Army Special Operations Command (USASOC) and the Joint Special Operations Command (JSOC).

Brian’s specific interest in Digital Assets (cryptocurrency), blockchain, Central Bank Digital Currencies and understanding conventional and emerging value transfer systems led to his final mission in the Army to pioneer, incubate, develop and employ financial capabilities to achieve understanding and end states both offensively and defensively.

He currently serves as an active faculty member for ACAMS - the Association of Certified Anti-Money Laundering Specialists.
